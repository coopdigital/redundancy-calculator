You’re not entitled to statutory redundancy pay.

You need to have worked for your employer continuously for at least 2 years before you can get statutory redundancy pay.

You do not have to accept your employer’s initial offer of redundancy.

There has to be a genuine reason that you have been selected for redundancy. This could include:

  • The business being shut down.
  • The requirements for a particular work for the business has ceased or diminished, meaning there is no need for the employer to keep your employment.

If you believe that the reason for your dismissal was not a redundancy or you have been offered a redundancy package we can advise you about your options.
